## 1. Pricing & Discount Strategy

- Many products have low discounts even though they are overstocked, which suggests the pricing strategy is not aligned with demand.  
- In some cases, products appear to have discounts, but the actual price reduction is minimal, making the discount ineffective.  
- Products with higher discounts and sufficient stock present a good opportunity for promotional campaigns.  

## 2. Inventory Analysis

- A number of products are overstocked but not selling efficiently, leading to unnecessary use of warehouse space.  
- Some high-value products are frequently out of stock, which indicates potential missed sales opportunities.  
- Inventory is not evenly distributed across categories, pointing to possible inefficiencies in stock planning.  

## 3. Revenue & Profitability

- Certain categories are generating strong revenue even without offering high discounts, indicating stable demand.  
- On the other hand, some categories hold a large amount of inventory but contribute relatively less to revenue.  
- Higher-priced products (above ₹500) tend to perform well, suggesting they have strong demand and better profit potential.  

## 4. Value for Money Analysis

- Bulk products generally offer a better price per gram, making them more attractive from a customer’s perspective.  
- There are noticeable differences in value across categories, which could influence purchasing decisions.  

## 5. Risk Identification

- Overstocked items tie up capital and increase storage costs, creating financial risk.  
- Products with low stock but high demand indicate lost sales opportunities.  
- Pricing inconsistencies for the same product suggest issues in data management or pricing control.  

## 6. Business Recommendations

### Pricing Strategy
- Adjust discounts for products that are not selling well to improve movement.  
- Re-evaluate pricing for high-demand items to maximize revenue.  

### Inventory Management
- Ensure timely restocking of high-demand products.  
- Reduce excess inventory in categories that are underperforming.  

### Marketing Strategy
- Focus promotions on products that have both high discounts and sufficient stock.  
- Emphasize bulk products as cost-effective options for customers.  

### Data Quality
- Address pricing inconsistencies across similar products.  
- Maintain accurate and consistent product data.  

## Final Conclusion

The analysis indicates a clear gap between pricing, inventory, and demand. This misalignment leads to missed revenue opportunities, inefficient use of inventory, and less effective pricing strategies.  

With better alignment and data-driven decisions, the business can improve overall performance in terms of revenue, inventory efficiency, and customer satisfaction.
